> [!NOTE]
> For this project, we designed and constructed a machine that takes in batter and produces pancakes. The machine dispenses batter onto a heated conveyor belt, which moves the batter towards the exit slot based on a timer. A heated top conveyor belt above the pancake works with the heated conveyor on the bottom to squeeze the pancake and dispense it through this slot. The machine is well-insulated, and Arduino-driven electrical work can be concentrated below or next to the product. A user-interface on the machine lets users know what is happening and what they need to do using an LED strip and a LCD display.

# PROJECT OVERVIEW

> [!IMPORTANT]
> We’re providing a solution for hungry, busy engineering students and teachers who lack time to make food. Students can come early, stay late, and stay on task without leaving the lab for food breaks. Being able to make pancakes efficiently with a machine will help the Engineering Lab and Robotics team raise money through sales.

## Design Goals

- 1 pancake every 60 seconds  
- 20 minutes continuous functionality minimum  
- Insulation  
- Electronics must be sealed  
- Consistent size  
- Effective user interface  
- Fully cooked  
- Minimize burnt pancakes and debris  

## Constraints

- Time: must be completed by April 26th  
- Budget: must be inside a $800.00 budget  
- Space: must fit in the lab  
- Safety: must be properly contained, so it will not be a fire or electrical hazard  
- Must weigh less than 25 kg

# CHALLENGES

- After choosing the project, conducting research was challenging, because there were not many existing automatic pancake makers to reference, and Popcake is very protective of product information.  
- Planning wiring and realizing that plan were both long and challenging iteration processes.  
- Designing the product was a challenging process. To avoid having to spend a large amount of time mastering CAD software, Jackson took advantage of his artistic skills to create technical drawings.  
- Ensuring user safety and maximizing cost efficiency were both challenging goals to achieve.

# EVALUATION & ANALYSIS

## REFLECTION

> Like many of the projects, this automatic pancake-maker was a struggle to make from start to finish. Neither of us could’ve done it alone. Because of this struggle, every accomplished task felt like it was worth celebrating, and enduring and adapting to the process head-on meant getting to learn everything the project required of us by ourselves. Above all, it was an amazing learning experience and successful collaboration.

## MISTAKES

- Taking our time at the start  
- Going long periods doing respective tasks without communicating progress to each other  
- Waiting until the last week to build and test the final product  
- Procrastinating on tasks that seemed too difficult to accomplish  
- Forgetting to set the potentiometer on electrical components, resulting in smoke, melting, and explosions on multiple occasions.  
- Underestimating the project’s difficulty  
- Avoiding using AI to debug until there was little time left to code.
